## Recipe 1: Anything But Basic Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/anything-but-basic-muffin-recipe/

**Yield:** 10 muffins
**Prep Time:** Not specified
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes (5 min at 425°F, then 15 min at 350°F)

### Ingredients
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 3/4 cup buttermilk
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 3/4 teaspoon salt

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line a muffin tin with 10 muffin cups and spray with oil-based spray.
2. In a large bowl, combine sugar, eggs, melted butter, buttermilk, and vanilla extract.
3. In a separate bowl, mix together flour, baking powder, and salt.
4. Fold dry ingredients into wet mixture, stirring only until combined. Having a few lumps in the batter is totally fine!
5. Scoop batter into prepared muffin cups, filling just shy of the top. Bake 5 minutes at 425°F, then lower oven to 350°F without opening the door and bake 15 additional minutes.
6. Cool in pan for 10 minutes, then transfer to wire rack to cool completely.

## Recipe 2: Fluffy Chocolate Chip Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/fluffy-chocolate-chip-muffins/

**Yield:** 11 muffins
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 2 eggs, room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 6 tablespoons Greek yogurt
- 6 tablespoons buttermilk
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 cup mini chocolate chips

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Prepare 10 standard muffin tins with liners.
2. In a large bowl, combine melted butter and sugar. Add eggs one at a time, then incorporate Greek yogurt, buttermilk, and vanilla extract.
3. Add flour, baking powder, and salt, mixing until just starting to combine. Fold in chocolate chips until just combined. Batter will be thick.
4. Scoop batter into prepared tins (about 3/4 full). Sprinkle tops with sugar if desired.
5. Bake at 425°F for 5 minutes, then reduce to 375°F and bake 15 additional minutes until golden brown.
6. Cool for at least 15 minutes before serving.

## Recipe 3: The Best Blueberry Muffins EVER

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/the-best-blueberry-muffins-ever/

**Yield:** 10 muffins
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**Total Time:** 35 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 2 large eggs, at room temperature
- 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- 1/3 cup Greek yogurt
- 1/3 cup milk (or buttermilk)
- 2 cups + 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 2 cups blueberries, fresh or frozen
- Turbinado sugar, optional

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Grease 10 standard muffin tins and line with cupcake liners.
2. Combine granulated sugar and melted butter in a large mixing bowl, whisking until combined.
3. Add eggs one at a time. Mix in Greek yogurt, milk, and vanilla extract.
4. Add flour, baking powder, and salt. Fold batter together with a silicone spatula until mostly combined—do not overmix. Toss blueberries in remaining 2 tablespoons flour, then fold into batter.
5. Scoop batter into prepared muffin tins, filling about 3/4 full. Sprinkle tops with turbinado sugar if desired.
6. Bake at 425°F for 5 minutes, then reduce oven to 375°F and bake an additional 15 minutes until golden brown.
7. Cool for at least 15 minutes before serving.

## Recipe 4: Pumpkin Streusel Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/pumpkin-streusel-muffins/

**Yield:** 12 muffins
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 15-17 minutes
**Total Time:** 30 minutes

### Ingredients - Muffins
- 1 1/2 cups pumpkin puree
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 1/2 cup brown sugar
- 1 3/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup vegetable oil
- 1/4 cup milk
- 2 eggs, room temperature
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
- 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- 1 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
- 1/2 teaspoon salt

### Ingredients - Streusel Topping
- 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup light brown sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup pecans, finely chopped

### Ingredients - Glaze
- 1/2 cup powdered sugar
- 2 teaspoons milk
- Splash of vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Line 12 muffin cups with liners and spray with cooking spray.
2. Combine pumpkin puree, sugars, vegetable oil, milk, eggs, and vanilla in a large bowl; mix well.
3. Add flour, baking powder, baking soda, cinnamon, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, and salt.
4. Stir dry ingredients into wet mixture until combined.
5. Distribute batter into prepared muffin cups, filling about two-thirds full.
6. Mix streusel ingredients until texture resembles wet sand. Divide evenly among the 12 muffins.
7. Bake 15-17 minutes until a knife inserted in center comes out mostly clean. Cool slightly.
8. Combine all glaze ingredients in a small bowl. Drizzle over cooled muffins and serve.

## Recipe 5: Blueberry Streusel Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/blueberry-streusel-muffins/

**Yield:** 10 muffins
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

### Ingredients - Muffins
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 2 eggs, room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 6 tablespoons Greek yogurt
- 6 tablespoons buttermilk
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 2 cups + 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2 cups blueberries (fresh or frozen)

### Ingredients - Streusel Topping
- 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1/3 cup granulated sugar
- 2/3 cup all-purpose flour

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Prepare 10 standard muffin tins with liners.
2. Combine streusel ingredients using a fork until texture resembles wet sand.
3. Mix melted butter and sugar. Add eggs one at a time, then yogurt, buttermilk, and vanilla.
4. Fold in flour, baking powder, and salt until barely combined. Toss blueberries with 2 tablespoons flour, then fold into batter.
5. Fill muffin cups 3/4 full using a large scoop.
6. Top each with approximately 2 tablespoons streusel, pressing gently.
7. Bake at 425°F for 5 minutes, then reduce to 375°F for 15 additional minutes until golden and springy.
8. Cool at least 15 minutes before serving.

## Recipe 6: Coffee Cake Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/coffee-cake-muffins/

**Yield:** 10 muffins
**Prep Time:** 25 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

### Ingredients - Muffin Batter
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 2 eggs, room temperature
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 1/4 cup buttermilk
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt

### Ingredients - Cinnamon Sugar Swirl
- 1/2 cup brown sugar
- 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- Pinch of salt

### Ingredients - Crumb Topping
- 1/3 cup brown sugar
- 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
- 6 tablespoons butter, melted

### Ingredients - Glaze
- 1 1/2 cups powdered sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt
- 2-3 tablespoons milk

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Grease and line 10 muffin tins with cupcake liners.
2. Combine melted butter and granulated sugar. Add eggs one at a time, then incorporate sour cream, buttermilk, and vanilla extract.
3. Add flour, baking powder, and salt; mix until partially combined.
4. In a separate bowl, combine cinnamon sugar swirl ingredients.
5. In another bowl, mix dry crumb topping ingredients, then add melted butter until clumpy.
6. Fill muffin cups halfway with batter. Add a spoonful of cinnamon mixture, then top with remaining batter (about 3/4 full).
7. Crown each muffin with crumb topping, pressing gently.
8. Bake 5 minutes at 425°F, then reduce to 375°F and bake 15 additional minutes until golden.
9. Cool 15 minutes. Mix glaze ingredients and drizzle over cooled muffins.

## Recipe 7: Chocolate Chunk Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/chocolate-chunk-muffins-recipe/

**Yield:** 9 large muffins
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**Total Time:** 30 minutes

### Ingredients
- 6 tablespoons butter, room temperature
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1 large egg, room temperature
- 3/4 cup non-fat Greek yogurt
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 1/2 cups flour
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 3/4 teaspoon salt
- 5 ounces dark chocolate, chopped

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Line 9 muffin tins with muffin cups.
2. Using a standing mixer with paddle attachment, cream butter and sugar on high until light and fluffy, about 3 minutes. Beat in egg for one minute, then add Greek yogurt and vanilla until combined.
3. In a small bowl, mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Incorporate into wet ingredients until partially combined (not fully incorporated). Fold chocolate chunks into batter until combined. Batter will be thick.
4. Scoop batter into muffin tins, filling just below the liner tops. Sprinkle granulated sugar on top.
5. Bake at 425°F for 5 minutes, then reduce oven to 375°F and bake an additional 15 minutes until golden brown and muffins spring back when touched.
6. Cool for at least 5 minutes before serving.

## Recipe 8: Blueberry Banana Cornbread Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/blueberry-banana-cornbread-muffins/

**Yield:** 12 muffins
**Prep Time:** 13 minutes
**Cook Time:** 17 minutes

### Ingredients
- 3 ripe bananas (approximately 1 1/4 cups mashed)
- 2 eggs
- 1/3 cup coconut oil, melted
- 2/3 cup sugar (coconut, brown, or white)
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 3/4 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 cup corn flour
- 3/4 cup gluten-free flour (Bob's 1-to-1 Baking Flour recommended)
- 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
- 1 cup fresh blueberries

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F and line a muffin tin with 12 cupcake liners.
2. Mash ripe bananas in a large bowl. Add eggs and coconut oil; mix well. Incorporate Greek yogurt, sugar, and vanilla extract, whisking to combine.
3. In a separate bowl, mix baking soda, corn flour, gluten-free flour, and salt. Fold dry ingredients into wet mixture until combined.
4. Fold in blueberries.
5. Divide batter evenly among 12 cups using a large cookie scoop or 1/4-cup measure.
6. Bake at 425°F for 5 minutes. Reduce heat to 375°F and bake 10-12 additional minutes until muffins spring back when touched.
7. Cool before serving.

**Note:** This recipe is gluten-free.

## Recipe 9: Lemon Blueberry Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/lemon-blueberry-muffins/

**Yield:** 10 muffins
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1 tablespoon lemon zest (from 1 lemon)
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 teaspoon lemon extract
- 1/3 cup Greek yogurt
- 1/3 cup milk
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 2 cups blueberries (fresh or frozen)
- Turbinado sugar (optional, for topping)

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Grease 10 muffin tins and line with cupcake liners.
2. Combine sugar and lemon zest in a large bowl. Rub together with your fingers until fragrant. Add melted butter and beat until combined.
3. Add eggs one at a time, then blend in Greek yogurt, milk, lemon juice, vanilla extract, and lemon extract. Mix thoroughly.
4. Fold in flour, baking powder, and salt until mostly combined—avoid overmixing. Gently fold in blueberries.
5. Distribute batter among prepared tins (about 3/4 full). Sprinkle with turbinado sugar if desired.
6. Bake at 425°F for 5 minutes, then reduce to 375°F and bake 15 minutes more until golden and springy.
7. Cool at least 15 minutes before serving.

## Recipe 10: Bakery Style Blueberry Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/bakery-style-blueberry-muffins/

**Yield:** 8 sky high muffins
**Prep Time:** Not specified
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes (5 min at 425°F, then 15 min at 375°F)

### Ingredients
- 1 1/2 cups flour
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 3/4 teaspoon salt
- 3/4 cup sugar
- 5 tablespoons butter, room temperature
- 1 large egg, room temperature
- 3/4 cup non-fat Greek yogurt
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 cup blueberries (fresh or frozen)
- Turbinado sugar for sprinkling (optional)

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F. Grease 8 large muffin tins and set aside.
2. Using a standing mixer with paddle attachment, cream butter and sugar on high for about 3 minutes until light and fluffy. Beat in egg for one minute, then incorporate Greek yogurt and vanilla extract until combined.
3. In a small bowl, mix flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Reserve 2 tablespoons of dry mixture. Add remaining flour blend to wet ingredients, mixing gently until partially combined. Toss blueberries with reserved flour, then fold into batter until just combined (batter will be thick).
4. Fill prepared muffin tins to the top. Sprinkle with turbinado sugar if desired.
5. Bake 5 minutes at 425°F, reduce heat to 375°F, and bake 15 additional minutes until golden brown and springy.
6. Cool at least 15 minutes before serving.

## Recipe 11: Healthy Blueberry Bran Muffins

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/blueberry-bran-breakfast-muffins/

**Yield:** 12 muffins
**Prep Time:** Not specified
**Cook Time:** 25-30 minutes

### Ingredients
- 2 cups wheat bran
- 1 cup raisins
- 1/2 cup water
- 3/4 cup white whole wheat flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup low fat buttermilk
- Fresh orange zest (a few swipes)
- 1/4 cup pure maple syrup (honey or agave as alternatives)
- 1/2 cup coconut oil, melted and cooled
- 2 large eggs
- 1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
2. Toast wheat bran on a baking sheet for 6-8 minutes, stirring occasionally; let cool.
3. Heat raisins with water, simmer 10 minutes until water absorbs, then puree until smooth.
4. Mix toasted bran, flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl; separately combine raisin puree, buttermilk, orange zest, maple syrup, coconut oil, and eggs.
5. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just combined, fold in blueberries.
6. Spoon 1/4 cup batter into each muffin tin, rounding the tops slightly.
7. Bake 25-30 minutes until muffins feel set in center.

**Note:** These freeze well for future use. Healthier option sweetened with maple syrup.
