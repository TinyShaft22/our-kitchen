## 25. Oreo Cheesecake Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/oreo-cheesecake-bars/

**Yield:** 16 squares
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 45 minutes
**Total Time:** ~2 hours (including cooling)

### Ingredients

**For the Oreo Crust:**
- 20 Oreo cookies, finely crushed
- 1/3 cup unsalted butter, melted
- Pinch of salt

**For the Cheesecake:**
- 2 packages (8 oz each) cream cheese, room temperature
- 1/2 cup sour cream, room temperature
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 16 Oreo cookies, finely crushed

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 325°F and line a 9x9 square pan with parchment paper.
2. Mix Oreo crumbs, melted butter, and salt until combined. Press firmly into pan as an even layer.
3. In a stand mixer with paddle attachment, beat softened cream cheese, sour cream, and sugar for 2 minutes until smooth and creamy.
4. Add vanilla extract, then add eggs one at a time, beating until just combined. Fold in crushed Oreo cookies.
5. Pour batter over crust. Drop pan on counter several times to remove air bubbles.
6. Bake for 45 minutes on middle rack, rotating halfway. Center will be slightly wobbly.
7. At 45 minutes, sprinkle additional Oreo pieces on top if desired. Turn off oven and open door slightly. Leave cheesecake in oven for 1 hour to cool gradually.
8. Remove and cool on rack for 1 hour to reach room temperature, then refrigerate until serving.
9. Slice into 16 squares using a sharp knife.

**Notes:** Ensure all ingredients are at room temperature; don't overbeat; allow gradual cooling.

## 26. The Best Chocolate Chip Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/the-best-chewy-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 25-30 minutes
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 large egg + 1 large egg yolk, room temperature
- 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 cup semisweet chocolate chips (optional)

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line an 8x8 pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk together melted butter and brown sugar in a large bowl. Add egg, egg yolk, and vanilla; mix well.
3. In a separate bowl, combine baking powder, salt, and flour. Fold dry ingredients into wet mixture until just combined. Fold in chocolate chips without overmixing.
4. Spread batter evenly into prepared pan.
5. Bake 25-30 minutes until golden brown with set edges. Center will be undercooked but continues cooking as it cools.
6. Cool completely before cutting into squares.

**Notes:** Don't overmix or overbake. Store in airtight container up to 7 days or freeze up to 2 months.

## 27. Lucky Charms Rice Krispie Treats

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/lucky-charms-rice-krispie-treats/

**Yield:** 16 bars
**Prep Time:** 5 minutes
**Cook Time:** 5 minutes
**Total Time:** 30 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/3 cup unsalted butter
- Pinch of salt
- 12 ounce bag of marshmallows
- 5 1/2 cups Lucky Charms cereal

### Instructions
1. Grease a 9x9 pan with cooking spray and set aside.
2. In a large pot over medium heat, melt the butter.
3. Add the marshmallows and salt; stir with a rubber spatula, scraping sides to prevent burning. Remove from heat immediately once marshmallows are melted.
4. Add the Lucky Charms cereal, folding to coat completely.
5. Spoon mixture into prepared pan, pressing down into an even layer.
6. Refrigerate for at least 20 minutes. Once set, cut into 12 rectangles using a sharp knife.

**Notes:** Spray rubber spatula with cooking spray if treats are especially sticky.

## 28. Chewy Funfetti Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/chewy-funfetti-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup butter, melted
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1 egg and 1 egg yolk
- 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 cup rainbow sprinkles

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line an 8x8 pan with parchment paper.
2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together melted butter and sugar. Add eggs, egg yolk, and vanilla extract; mix well.

4. Pour batter into prepared pan, spreading into an even layer.

6. Once cooled, cut into 9 large or 16 smaller squares.

**Notes:** Avoid overmixing for tender crumb. Use high-quality butter and vanilla.

## 29. Bourbon Snickerdoodle Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/bourbon-snickerdoodle-blondies/

**Yield:** 9 large blondies
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 35 minutes
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled slightly
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1 egg, room temperature
- 2 tablespoons bourbon whiskey
- 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 3/4 teaspoons cream of tartar
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon granulated sugar (for topping)
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon (for topping)

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease and line an 8x8 baking pan with parchment paper.
2. In a standing mixer with paddle attachment, cream melted butter and sugar on high speed for 30 seconds. Add egg and bourbon; mix for another 30 seconds.
3. In a separate bowl, combine flour, cream of tartar, and salt. Fold dry ingredients into wet mixture until combined. Place into prepared pan.
4. Mix together sugar and cinnamon; sprinkle over batter. Bake for 30-35 minutes until a knife in center comes out mostly clean. Cool completely before cutting into squares.

**Notes:** Cream of tartar provides rise and snickerdoodle tanginess. Store 3-4 days or freeze up to 3 months.

## 30. White Chocolate Cranberry Crumb Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/cranberry-crumb-bars/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 30-40 minutes
**Total Time:** 1 hour

### Ingredients

**For the Cookie Bar:**
- 2/3 cup old fashioned oats
- 2 1/2 cups all purpose flour
- 2/3 cup granulated sugar
- 2/3 cup brown sugar, packed
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 3/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
- 1 egg, room temperature
- 2 tablespoons milk
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

**For the Cranberry Layer:**
- 4 cups fresh whole cranberries
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 tablespoon orange zest
- Pinch of salt

**For White Chocolate Drizzle:**
- 1/2 cup white chocolate chips, melted

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F; line 9x9 pan with parchment paper
2. Combine oats, flour, sugars, baking soda, and salt
3. Cut cold butter into dry mixture until resembling wet sand
4. Make well in center; add egg and milk; mix until dough forms
5. Press 2/3 of mixture into prepared pan
6. Combine all cranberry layer ingredients; spread over crust
7. Sprinkle remaining dough over cranberries; pat gently
8. Bake 30-40 minutes until crust is golden and cranberries bubble
9. Cool completely before drizzling with white chocolate and slicing

**Notes:** Store at room temperature up to 7 days, refrigerated 2 weeks, or frozen 2 months.

## 31. Browned Butter Mummy Rice Krispie Treats

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/browned-butter-mummy-rice-krispie-treats/

**Yield:** 12 rectangles
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Chill Time:** 40 minutes
**Total Time:** 55 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/3 cup unsalted butter
- Pinch of salt
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 16 ounces bag of marshmallows
- 5 cups Rice Krispies cereal
- 2 cups white chocolate chips
- 1 teaspoon coconut oil
- Sprinkle googly eyes

### Instructions
1. Grease a 9x9 pan with cooking spray and set aside
2. Brown the butter over medium heat, stirring constantly until golden brown with nutty aroma
3. Add marshmallows and salt; stir with rubber spatula, scraping sides. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla
4. Add Rice Krispies, folding to coat completely
5. Spoon mixture into prepared pan, pressing down evenly
6. Refrigerate 20 minutes, then cut into 12 rectangles
7. Melt white chocolate and coconut oil in 15-second microwave increments
8. Dip treats 2/3 way into white chocolate; place googly eyes
9. Pipe white chocolate drizzles over treats to create mummy effect
10. Refrigerate 20 minutes to set

**Notes:** Use greased spatula or wet hands for easier pressing.

## 32. Maple Chai Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/maple-chai-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 servings (or 8 large)
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Total Time:** 1 hour

### Ingredients

**For the Blondies:**
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 3/4 cup brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons pure maple syrup
- 1 egg, room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon ground cardamom
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon ginger
- 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg
- Pinch of black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/3 cup toasted, chopped walnuts

**For the Maple Glaze:**
- 3/4 cup powdered sugar
- 1 tablespoon pure maple syrup
- 2 tablespoons heavy cream or milk
- Pinch of salt

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line an 8x8 pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk together melted butter, maple syrup, and brown sugar. Add egg and vanilla; mix well.
3. In separate bowl, stir together baking powder, spices, salt, and flour. Fold wet ingredients into dry until just combined. Fold in toasted nuts. Do not overmix.
4. Pour batter into prepared pan, spreading evenly.
5. Bake 30 minutes until golden brown and edges are set; middle will remain undercooked.
6. While cooling, whisk together powdered sugar, maple syrup, cream, and salt until creamy.
7. Pour glaze over cooled blondies. Allow to harden before cutting into squares.

## 33. Peanut Butter and Jelly Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/peanut-butter-and-jelly-bars/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Total Time:** 40 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
- 3/4 cup creamy peanut butter
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 2 cups all purpose flour
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 3/4 cup jam (any flavor)

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line a 9x9 pan with parchment on all sides.
2. In a stand mixer with paddle attachment, combine butter, peanut butter, sugar, flour, salt, and vanilla. Beat until dough starts to come together, about 2 minutes. Dough will be crumbly.
3. Press 2/3 of shortbread dough into prepared pan. Top with jam spread into an even layer. Crumble remaining dough over jam, leaving some jam visible. Bake 25-30 minutes until golden brown.
4. Allow to cool before cutting into desired sizes.

**Notes:** Dough should be crumbly—don't overmix. Use high-quality jam.

## 34. Carrot Cake Cheesecake Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/carrot-cake-cheesecake-bars/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

### Ingredients

**For the carrot cake:**
- 1 1/4 cup brown sugar
- 3/4 cup butter, melted
- 1 egg + 1 egg yolk
- 1 1/2 cup grated carrots
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 1/4 cups all purpose flour
- 1 1/2 teaspoons cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon orange zest (optional)

**For the cheesecake swirl:**
- 4 ounces cream cheese, room temperature
- 1 egg, room temperature
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F and line a 9x9 square pan with parchment paper. Combine brown sugar and butter in a mixing bowl; whisk to combine. Add vanilla, eggs, and carrots; mix.
2. Add flour, cinnamon, baking powder, salt, and orange zest; mix until just combined with no flour streaks. Do not overmix.
3. Pour carrot batter into prepared pan and set aside while preparing the cheesecake layer.
4. Combine softened cream cheese, egg, granulated sugar, and vanilla; beat until creamy and homogenous. Don't overmix.
5. Drop dollops of cheesecake batter on carrot batter; use a butter knife to make big swirls. Bake at 350°F for 30 minutes or until a knife inserted in the middle comes out mostly clean.
6. Allow bars to cool completely before slicing into squares and serving.

## 35. Peanut Butter S'mores Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/peanut-butter-smores-bars/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/4 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
- 1/2 cup natural peanut butter
- 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 large egg, room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 3/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 3/4 cup graham cracker crumbs
- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 3/4 teaspoon salt
- 2/3 cup chocolate (chopped Hershey's bars or similar)
- 2/3 cup mini marshmallows

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F; line 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper.
2. In stand mixer with paddle attachment, combine butter and peanut butter; beat on medium until homogeneous.
3. Add brown sugar and mix well; scrape bowl, then add egg and vanilla extract.
4. Scrape bowl again; add flour, graham cracker crumbs, baking soda, and salt; mix until combined.
5. Fold in marshmallows and chocolate; transfer to prepared pan in even layer.
6. Bake 25-30 minutes until edges are set and knife inserted in center comes out mostly clean; cool before slicing.

**Notes:** Avoid overmixing and slightly underbake for optimal gooey texture.

## 36. Zingy Ginger Molasses Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/zingy-ginger-molasses-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 35 minutes
**Total Time:** 50 minutes

### Ingredients

**For the blondies:**
- 1 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
- 1 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
- 4 tablespoons molasses
- 2 eggs, room temperature
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 3/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 2 teaspoons ground ginger
- 1 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon cloves
- 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, finely chopped
- 2 1/4 cups all purpose flour

**For the topping:**
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 4 ounces white chocolate, finely chopped

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F; line 9x9 pan with parchment paper
2. Whisk together melted butter, brown sugar, and molasses until combined
3. Add eggs, vanilla, and fresh ginger; mix to incorporate
4. In separate bowl, sift together flour, ginger, cinnamon, cloves, baking powder, and salt
5. Fold dry ingredients into wet mixture without overmixing
6. Combine granulated sugar, cinnamon, and salt for topping
7. Spread batter evenly in prepared pan; sprinkle cinnamon sugar topping, patting slightly
8. Bake 20-30 minutes until edges set but center remains slightly gooey
9. Cool completely, then drizzle with melted white chocolate; allow 30 minutes to set before slicing

**Notes:** Underbaking produces a gooier texture; fresh ginger adds extra flavor depth.
