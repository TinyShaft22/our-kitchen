## 1. Gluten Free Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/gluten-free-blondies-recipe/

**Yield:** 9 large blondies (or 16 smaller ones)
**Prep Time:** 10 minutes
**Cook Time:** 25-30 minutes
**Total Time:** 40 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 large egg + 1 large egg yolk, room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup gluten-free flour (Bob's Red Mill 1:1 or King Arthur recommended)
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup dark chocolate chips

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk melted butter and brown sugar together until combined.
3. Whisk in egg, extra yolk, and vanilla.
4. Add flour, baking powder, and salt. Fold with spatula until just combined (no flour streaks). Gently fold in chocolate chips.
5. Scrape batter into prepared pan, spreading to edges.
6. Bake 25-30 minutes until edges are set and golden brown. Center may look underbaked.
7. Cool completely in pan on rack. Use parchment paper to lift out, then cut and serve.

**Notes:** Don't overbake—the middle should appear slightly underbaked as they continue cooking while cooling.

## 2. Caramel Apple Cheesecake Crumble Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/caramel-apple-cheesecake-crumble-bars/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 40 minutes
**Cook Time:** 35 minutes
**Total Time:** 4 hours

### Ingredients

**For the crust and crumb topping:**
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1/3 cup granulated sugar
- 1/3 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1/2 teaspoon salt

**For the cheesecake layer:**
- 16 ounces cream cheese, room temperature
- 1/4 cup sour cream, room temperature
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 2 large eggs, room temperature
- 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup salted caramel (optional topping)

**For the apple layer:**
- 1 medium apple, peeled, cored, diced
- 1 tablespoon packed light brown sugar
- 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 325°F; line 9x9-inch pan with parchment paper.
2. Mix melted butter, sugars, flour, spices, and salt until resembling wet sand. Press half into pan bottom firmly; reserve remaining mixture.
3. Beat cream cheese, sour cream, and sugar until smooth (30 seconds).
4. Scrape bowl sides; add eggs, vanilla, and salt. Beat until smooth (45 seconds).
5. Transfer batter to prepared pan; smooth evenly.
6. Combine apple pieces with brown sugar and cinnamon; sprinkle over cheesecake. Press gently into batter. Crumble reserved mixture over apples.
7. Bake 35-40 minutes until puffed and set at edges with slight wobble in center.
8. Turn off oven; crack door open. Leave cheesecake inside 30 minutes to cool gradually.
9. Run knife along edges; transfer pan to refrigerator. Chill minimum 2 hours or overnight.
10. Remove from refrigerator; drizzle with salted caramel, slice, and serve.

**Notes:** Store in airtight container up to 4 days. Freeze without caramel up to 2 months.

## 3. Pumpkin Snickerdoodle Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/pumpkin-snickerdoodle-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 20 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**Total Time:** 40 minutes

### Ingredients

**For the blondies:**
- 1/2 cup pumpkin puree, dried
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 1 large egg yolk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 1/4 teaspoon cream of tartar
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg

**For cinnamon sugar coating:**
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1 tablespoon ground cinnamon
- Pinch of salt

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F; line 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper
2. Blot pumpkin puree on paper towels; squeeze to remove excess moisture
3. Combine dried pumpkin, melted butter, both sugars; whisk together
4. Add egg yolk and vanilla; mix well
5. Add flour, baking soda, cream of tartar, salt, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg; mix until combined
6. Combine cinnamon sugar ingredients; sprinkle over batter
7. Bake 18-22 minutes until edges set but center slightly gooey
8. Cool before eating

**Notes:** The drying step for pumpkin puree is crucial—it removes excess moisture, preventing a cakey texture.

## 4. Cookie Dough Ice Cream Sandwiches

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/cookie-dough-ice-cream-sandwiches/

**Yield:** 12 servings
**Prep Time:** 45 minutes
**Cook Time:** 7 minutes
**Chill Time:** 3 hours
**Total Time:** 4 hours

### Ingredients
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 3/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 2 tablespoons milk
- 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup mini chocolate chips
- 1 quart vanilla ice cream, slightly softened

### Instructions
1. Heat-treat flour at 350°F for 7-8 minutes until it reaches 165°F internally, stirring halfway through. Cool completely.
2. Line two 8x8-inch square pans with parchment paper on all sides.
3. Combine melted butter, brown sugar, granulated sugar, milk, and vanilla extract in a large bowl using a rubber spatula.
4. Add cooled flour and salt; fold until no streaks remain. Add chocolate chips and fold to combine.
5. Divide dough in half, spreading each into an even layer in prepared pans. Freeze for 30 minutes.
6. Spread softened ice cream over one chilled dough layer. Remove second dough slab from freezer, invert onto ice cream, and press gently to seal. Cover with plastic wrap and freeze at least 3 hours.
7. Remove from freezer and slice into desired-sized sandwiches.

**Notes:** The cookie dough is heat-treated for food safety. Ice cream should be slightly softened for easy spreading but not melted.

## 5. S'mores Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/smores-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 22 minutes
**Total Time:** 37 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 large egg at room temperature
- 1 tablespoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 3/4 cup graham cracker crumbs
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- Pinch of cinnamon (optional)
- 1 1/2 cups marshmallow fluff
- 4.4 oz Hershey's chocolate bar or 1 XL bar, broken into pieces

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line an 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper on all sides.
2. Whisk melted butter and brown sugar together. Add egg and vanilla, mixing until glossy.
3. Fold in flour, graham cracker crumbs, baking powder, salt, and cinnamon.
4. Transfer half the batter to the prepared pan, spreading evenly to edges.
5. Spread marshmallow fluff over bottom layer using a greased spatula.
6. Form remaining dough into flat discs, placing over fluff to cover most of it. Press chocolate pieces on top.
7. Bake 20-25 minutes until golden brown and firm to touch. Cool slightly before slicing.

**Notes:** Best consumed fresh or within 3 days when stored in an airtight container at room temperature.

## 6. Tiramisu Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/tiramisu-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 1 hour
**Cook Time:** 20-25 minutes
**Total Time:** 2 hours

### Ingredients

**For the Blondies:**
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 1 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 large egg + 1 large egg yolk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 tablespoon espresso powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder

**For the Espresso Soak:**
- 1/4 cup warm water
- 1 tablespoon espresso powder
- 1 teaspoon cocoa powder

**For the Tiramisu Cream:**
- 1 cup heavy whipping cream
- 8 ounces mascarpone (cold)
- 1 cup powdered sugar
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- Pinch of salt
- Cocoa powder for dusting

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk melted butter and brown sugar together. Add egg, egg yolk, and vanilla; mix well.
3. Fold in flour, espresso powder, salt, and baking powder until just combined.
4. Spread batter evenly in prepared pan.
5. Bake 20-25 minutes until golden brown and set on edges; center remains slightly undercooked.
6. Combine warm water, espresso powder, and cocoa powder. Brush mixture onto cooled blondies.
7. In stand mixer, beat heavy cream, mascarpone, powdered sugar, vanilla, and salt until stiffened (about 1 minute).
8. Spread mascarpone cream over blondies in even layer. Dust generously with cocoa powder.
9. Refrigerate 30 minutes before slicing and serving.

## 7. The Best Carmelitas

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/the-best-carmelitas/

**Yield:** 16 bars
**Prep Time:** 40 minutes
**Cook Time:** 30 minutes
**Total Time:** 1 hour 10 minutes

### Ingredients

**For the base and topping:**
- 1 cup unsalted butter, browned
- 1 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 1/3 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 1/3 cups old fashioned oats
- 3/4 teaspoon baking soda
- 3/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 large egg yolk
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 1 cup semi-sweet or dark chocolate chips

**For the caramel layer:**
- 40 soft caramels (Werther's recommended)
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1/4 teaspoon salt

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F; line 9x9-inch pan with parchment paper
2. Brown butter over medium heat until golden and nutty; cool slightly
3. Combine brown sugar, flour, oats, baking soda, and salt; add browned butter, egg yolk, and vanilla; stir until dough forms
4. Add chocolate chips; transfer half the dough to prepared pan
5. Melt caramels with heavy cream over low-medium heat; stir in salt and vanilla
6. Pour caramel over bottom layer; press remaining dough on top
7. Bake 30 minutes until golden brown; cool completely before slicing

**Notes:** Features brown butter for a nutty, caramel-like depth. Store in airtight container at room temperature up to 3 days or refrigerated for a week.

## 8. Baklava Blondies

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/baklava-blondies/

**Yield:** 16 blondies
**Prep Time:** 1 hour
**Cook Time:** 25 minutes
**Total Time:** 3 hours 25 minutes

### Ingredients

**For the Blondies:**
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
- 3/4 cup packed light brown sugar
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 1 egg + 1 egg yolk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/3 cup pistachios, chopped
- 1/3 cup walnuts, chopped

**For the Honey Caramel:**
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1/3 cup honey
- 1 lemon rind strip
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice
- 3 tablespoons heavy cream
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- Heavy pinch of salt
- 1/3 cup pistachios, chopped
- 1/3 cup walnuts, chopped

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk melted butter, brown sugar, and honey together. Add egg, egg yolk, and vanilla; whisk to combine.
3. Fold in flour, baking powder, and salt until combined. Gently fold in chopped nuts.
4. Transfer batter to prepared pan and bake 25 minutes until golden brown.
5. While baking, combine butter and honey in saucepan over medium heat with lemon rind. Simmer until mixture reduces by half (about 15 minutes). Add cream, lemon juice, cinnamon, and salt; cook 10 more minutes until thickened.
6. Sprinkle nuts over cooled blondies and pour caramel sauce over top. Refrigerate at least 2 hours until set.
7. Cut into squares (or triangles) with a sharp knife. Serve.

## 9. Brown Sugar Peach Crumb Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/brown-sugar-peach-crumb-bars/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 20 minutes
**Cook Time:** 40 minutes
**Total Time:** 1 hour

### Ingredients

**For the Peach Filling:**
- 4 ripe peaches, pitted and sliced
- 1/3 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon (optional)

**For the Crumb Bars:**
- 3/4 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
- 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
- 2 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
- 1 2/3 cups all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon baking powder

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line an 8x8-inch square pan with parchment paper on all sides.
2. Prepare filling: Pit and slice peaches into 1/4-inch thick slices. Heat a heavy saucepan over medium heat and combine 2/3 of the sliced peaches with brown sugar, flour, and cinnamon. Stir and cook 10 minutes until peaches break down into jam. Add uncooked peaches and stir to combine. Set aside.
3. Make crumb dough: In a stand mixer with paddle attachment, combine softened butter, brown sugar, vanilla, flour, salt, and baking powder. Mix on low speed about 3 minutes until mixture clumps together and forms dough, scraping bowl sides as needed.
4. Press approximately 2/3 of mixture into prepared pan using fingers or measuring cup bottom to create even layer. Spread peach jam over crust to edges. Sprinkle remaining topping in small and large clusters over peaches.
5. Bake 35-40 minutes until peaches bubble and top is golden brown. Cool completely on rack before slicing and serving.

**Notes:** Store in refrigerator in an airtight container up to 5 days.

## 10. Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/strawberry-rhubarb-crumble-bars/

**Yield:** 16-24 servings (9x13-inch pan)
**Prep Time:** 40 minutes
**Cook Time:** 25-30 minutes
**Total Time:** 1 hour 10 minutes

### Ingredients

**For the Strawberry Rhubarb Jam:**
- 2 cups rhubarb, cut into 1/4-inch pieces
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch
- 1 quart strawberries, washed, hulled, and quartered

**For the Crumble:**
- 1 cup unsalted butter, room temperature
- 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
- 1 large egg, room temperature
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- Zest of 1/2 orange
- 3 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon salt

### Instructions
1. Combine rhubarb, sugar, and cornstarch in a saucepan over medium heat. Cook 5 minutes until rhubarb breaks down, then add strawberries. Cook 8 more minutes until jammy. Cool.
2. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line 9x13-inch pan with parchment paper.
3. Beat butter and sugar until combined. Add egg, vanilla, and orange zest. Mix in flour, baking powder, and salt until crumbly dough forms.
4. Press 2/3 of dough into prepared pan as crust. Spread jam evenly over crust. Crumble remaining dough into clusters and sprinkle over jam.
5. Bake 25-30 minutes until jam bubbles at edges and top is golden brown. Cool completely before slicing.

**Notes:** Store tightly covered in the refrigerator or freeze up to 2 months.

## 11. Cowboy Cookie Bars

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/cowboy-cookie-bars/

**Yield:** 16 bars
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 25-30 minutes
**Total Time:** 45 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted and cooled
- 1 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1 egg + 1 egg yolk, room temperature
- 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
- 3/4 cup all purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup old fashioned oats
- 1/2 cup shredded coconut
- 1/2 cup semisweet chocolate chips
- 1/3 cup pecans, roughly chopped

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Line 8x8-inch pan with parchment paper.
2. Whisk melted butter and brown sugar together. Add egg, yolk, and vanilla; mix well.
3. Fold in flour, cinnamon, baking powder, and salt until just combined.
4. Fold in oats, coconut, chocolate chips, and pecans until distributed evenly.
5. Transfer batter to prepared pan, spreading to edges.
6. Bake 25-30 minutes until golden brown and set on edges; center remains slightly undercooked.
7. Cool completely, then cut into 9 or 16 squares as desired.

**Notes:** Store in airtight container in refrigerator for days; can be frozen up to 3 months.

## 12. 7 Layer Bar Rice Krispie Treats

**URL:** https://bromabakery.com/7-layer-bar-rice-krispie-treats/

**Yield:** 16 servings
**Prep Time:** 20 minutes
**Cook Time:** 5 minutes
**Chill Time:** 1 hour
**Total Time:** 1 hour 25 minutes

### Ingredients
- 1/2 cup shredded coconut
- 5 cups Rice Krispies cereal
- 16 ounces marshmallows
- 1/3 cup unsalted butter
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 cup graham cracker pieces
- 1/2 cup butterscotch chips
- 1/2 cup chocolate chips
- 1/2 cup walnuts, finely chopped

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F; line baking sheet with parchment paper. Spread coconut in even layer and bake 5-7 minutes, stirring midway.
2. Line 9x9-inch pan with parchment on all sides; grease with cooking spray. Pour Rice Krispies and toasted coconut into large mixing bowl; set aside.
3. Melt butter in medium saucepan over medium-low heat. Add marshmallows, vanilla extract, and salt; cook, stirring occasionally, until completely melted. Remove from heat.
4. Pour melted marshmallow mixture into cereal bowl. Fold together until evenly combined. Fold in graham crackers, butterscotch chips, chocolate chips, and walnuts (reserve some for topping).
5. Transfer mixture to prepared pan. Use wet spatula to pat into even layer. Top with reserved toppings. Refrigerate 20 minutes, then cut into 16 squares.

**Notes:** Store in airtight container in refrigerator up to 9 days or on counter up to 5 days.
